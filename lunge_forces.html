The physics

Vertical landing speed from the center-of-mass drop h:

vvert = √(2 · g · h)

Total velocity the front leg must arrest (forward + vertical):

Δv = √( (f · vfwd)² + vvert² )

Average force from impulse–momentum + body weight (Newton II):

Favg = m · Δv / Δt + m · g

Peak force, joint loads:

Fpeak = k · Favg   Fknee = Fpeak · a(θ)   Fankle ≈ Fpeak

Model & assumptions

  • g = 9.81 m/s². Body weight BW = m·g.
  • f = 0.40 — fraction of forward momentum arrested by the front leg on contact (the rest carries through the lunge).
  • k = 1.5 — peak-to-average factor for a roughly triangular impact pulse.
  • Knee amplification a(θ) = 1 + 0.9·(1 − cos θ) — quadriceps/patellar load rises with knee flexion; θ is mapped from lunge depth (≈30° shallow → ≈110° deep).
  • Ankle sees essentially the full ground reaction force (distal foot mass is negligible during impact). Segment masses follow standard anthropometric tables (Winter).
  • Single rigid point-mass impact model — no soft-tissue, muscle co-contraction dynamics, or multi-segment inverse dynamics.
Educational estimate, not a clinical measurement. This is a simplified classical-mechanics model built to show how lunge parameters influence joint impact loads — the qualitative trends (stiffer/faster/deeper landings raise joint force) are robust, but absolute values are approximate. A validated study would use force plates and multi-segment inverse dynamics.
